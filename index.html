Wine guide

Wine, explained simply.

A few simple answers about wine: what sets the styles apart, why wine tastes sweet or dry and how it is made in the first place.

What is Lennart’s Wein?

+

Lennart’s Wein is a personal wine guide by Lennart with simple, honest recommendations for dinner, gifts, summer evenings and beginner-friendly bottles — chosen by mood, food and occasion rather than complicated wine language.

What is the difference between red, white and sparkling wine?

+

Red wine ferments together with the grape skins, which adds colour and tannin. White wine is usually made without the skins, so it stays lighter and crisper. Sparkling wine gets a second fermentation that traps gas and creates its bubbles.

Why are some wines sweet and others dry?

+

Sweetness comes from the natural grape sugar left after fermentation. If the yeast turns nearly all of it into alcohol, the wine tastes dry. When some sugar remains, the wine tastes off dry or sweet.

How do you choose your wines?

+

The selection is personal rather than complete. The focus is on calm, balanced wines with their own quiet character — a small, curated choice instead of a long list, always picked the way I would recommend them to a friend.

Which wine is good for beginners?

+

If you are new to wine, start with something soft and approachable: a fruity white, a light rosé or a smooth, easy-drinking red. Beginner-friendly bottles are balanced and forgiving, so you can simply enjoy them without thinking about the details.

How is wine made?

+

Wine is made by fermenting grape juice, where yeast turns the natural sugar into alcohol. Grapes are harvested, pressed and fermented, then often aged before bottling. Small choices at each step shape the final taste.
